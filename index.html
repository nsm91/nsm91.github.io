Work

GIS Intern at the Field Museum, Chicago IL

My time at the field focused on two projects. The primary project was designing a geospatial database for the Right-of-way as Habitat Working Group through UIC. The working group focused getting various entities with right-of-way land (utility companies, railroads, transportation agencies, etc.) to engage in in pollinator conservation on those lands. The Field Museum was tasked with creating a geospatial database to record those efforts and increase the efficiency of monitoring those site. Additionally I worked on another project that used lidar data to extract tree top points to try and inventory entire swathes of forested land through remote sensing alone.


GIS Graduate Assistant

At Northeastern Illinois University I was technical and teaching assistant for the college of Geography and Evironmental Studies. My duties included assisting during class, grading assignments, and hosting office hours several dats a week.
I found it a very rewarding experience in the field of education, as well continuing to hone my GIS skills.


GIS Consultant for the Burr Ridge Public Works Department

After my dual Forestry and GIS internship, I stayed on with Burr Ridge part-time to continue managing their GIS needs. This includes managing their online GIS web portal and managing all municiple geospatial data. I am also intimately involved in the annual Ash tree assessments, maintianing the ever-changing tree inventory.
